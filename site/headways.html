CTA Frequent Network Analysis

The Headway Reality Check

CTA promises 10-minute headways on Frequent Network routes. We tracked real-time bus positions to measure what riders actually experience.

Preliminary data. These results are based on approximately 99 hours of real-time data collection (Feb 11–16, 2026). Collection runs every 30 minutes via GitHub Actions and this page updates daily. Robust conclusions require at least two weeks of continuous monitoring. Treat these as early indicators, not definitive findings.

Schedule vs. Reality

CTA schedules 97–100% of Frequent Network headways at 10 minutes or less. In our observations, only about 59% of headways actually met that promise (filtered to the service window). The gap between what's planned and what riders experience is significant across nearly every route.

How We Measured

We polled CTA's Bus Tracker API every 60 seconds for real-time vehicle positions on all 20 Frequent Network routes. Stop arrivals are detected when a vehicle comes within 30 meters of a reference stop (selected at the midpoint of each route). Headways are computed as the time between consecutive arrivals at the same stop traveling in the same direction.

Scheduled headways

Scheduled headways come from the CTA's GTFS static feed, which provides planned stop times for each trip. We computed the time between consecutive scheduled arrivals at the same reference stop and filtered to the Frequent Network service window.

Service window

The Frequent Network promises 10-minute headways during specific hours:

  • Weekdays: 6:00 AM – 9:00 PM
  • Weekends: 9:00 AM – 9:00 PM

Both scheduled and observed headways are filtered to these windows for comparison.

Data collection

Vehicle positions are collected automatically every 30 minutes via GitHub Actions and stored in Cloudflare D1. This page updates daily with the latest analysis. Currently approximately 99 hours of real-time collection (Feb 11–16, 2026).

Limitations

  • Growing dataset. Data is collected automatically every 30 minutes during service hours and accumulates over time. Earlier results may be based on limited samples. At minimum, two weeks of continuous data across weekdays and weekends are needed for robust conclusions.
  • Reference stop selection. We use a single reference stop per route (the midpoint). Headway performance may vary at different points along a route — buses can bunch or spread out as they travel.
  • Arrival detection. The 30-meter proximity threshold for detecting arrivals may occasionally miss stops or double-count vehicles that linger near a stop.
